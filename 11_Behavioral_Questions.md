# Data Analyst Interview - Behavioral & Soft Skills

## Tell me about yourself
Structure: Present → Past → Future
"I'm a data analyst with X years specializing in SQL, Excel, and Power BI. Recently, I [specific achievement with metric]. I'm excited about this role because..."

## Why this company?
- Research their products, mission, recent news
- Connect your skills to their needs
- Show genuine interest

## Describe a challenging project
Use STAR: Situation, Task, Action, Result
Focus on data challenges, technical solutions, business impact

## How do you handle conflicting priorities?
- Assess urgency and impact
- Communicate with stakeholders
- Document decisions

## Explain a complex analysis to a non-technical audience
- Use analogies
- Focus on business impact
- Use visuals over numbers
- Check for understanding

## What is your approach to data quality?
- Validate at source
- Document assumptions
- Build in checks
- Communicate limitations

## How do you stay current?
- Online courses (Coursera, DataCamp)
- Blogs and newsletters
- Practice projects
- Professional communities

## Strengths?
- Analytical thinking
- Attention to detail
- Communication
- Problem-solving

## Weaknesses? (Choose something you're improving)
- "I sometimes dive too deep into analysis. I'm learning to set time boxes."
- "I'm working on my Python skills to complement my SQL expertise."

## Questions to ask them:
- What does success look like in this role at 6 months?
- What tools and data stack does the team use?
- What's the biggest challenge the team is facing?
- How is the data team structured?
